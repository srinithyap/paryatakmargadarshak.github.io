HYDERABAD SAFTEY


Hyderabad, city, Telangana state, south-central India. It is Telangana’s largest and most-populous city and is the major urban centre for all of south-central interior India.Hyderabad is popular with tourists due to its numerous temples, bazaars, museums.

WARNINGS & DANGERS IN HYDERABAD


OVERALL RISK: MEDIUM


Hyderabad is filled with new technology, good food, and fashionable trends. However, try to avoid buying items on the street, as they might not be what they appear.In this city, you need to take special precautions, watch out for your belongings and surroundings, and do not walk alone late at night.

TRANSPORT & TAXIS RISK : LOW

In this city, you need to take special precautions, watch out for your belongings and surroundings, and do not walk alone late at night.

PICK POCKETS RISK : MEDIUM


Pickpocketing occurs in Hyderabad at every turn, so wear an anti-theft backpack, look after your belongings, and don't leave your phones and wallets unattended. In crowded places, keep an eye on your surroundings.It may involve considerable dexterity and a knack for misdirection.Pay close attention to your belongings at all time. Just because your bags are on your body doesn’t mean they are safe. Keep locks on your backpack zippers. Keep your purse IN FRONT of you—not on the side—so you can see it at all times.Be cautious of people who are constantly bumping into you. Often times pickpockets work in pairs or groups.

MUGGING RISK : LOW

he risk of serious robbery and armed attacks is shallow in Hyderabad. There is little danger of these attacks in the suburbs. However, the likelihood that you will be attacked is very small.

TERRORISM RISK : LOW

The risk of terrorism is low in Hyderabad. Of course, the risk of attacks still exists due to the international position of the city. The global risk of terrorist attacks in public areas exists worldwide, so tourists should be vigilant.

SCAMS RISK : HIGH


Fraud is commonly associated with street vendors offering counterfeit goods to tourists. Don't buy them even if the price is very attractive. Also, only withdraw money from ATMs at banks with cameras to avoid skimming your card.

WOMEN TRAVELERS RISK : MEDIUM


Female travellers may be more liable to experience sexual har assment or assault in some locations, as well as opportunistic crime, kidnap or targeted attack. Spotlight on safety.Women traveling alone in this city should avoid late-night walks and be very discreet on the city streets. Heightened precautions won't hurt. Wear modest clothing that covers your knees and shoulders.

SO... HOW SAFE IS HYDERABAD REALLY?

Like most parts of India, Hyderabad is a fairly safe metropolis. Robberies and violent crimes are rare; most crimes involve theft. Tourists need to keep a close eye on their belongings and not leave them unattended. Hyderabad is a relatively safe city to travel to. The crime index here is at an average level.
